Noun Adjective

Section 1: The basics and freebies

Each question has a latin noun and adjective. You can tell which is the noun by the text at the bottom: whichever one has the gender abreviation letter (f. m. or n.). In this case, you can tell that Solomonem is the noun because in the little text at the bottom, it has a m. next to it. The other word is going to be the adjective. All nouns and adjectives have three characteristics that have to agree/be the same: number case and gender. The following are all the possible numbers cases and genders.

Gender Case Number
Feminine Nominative Singular
Masculine (m.) Genitive Plural
Neuter (n.) Dative
Accusative
Ablative

All three of these must agree for you to put yes. Put no if any one doesn't agree

The following endings will always agree no matter what. You should still be careful when looking at these however because -is is different from -īs

-īs
-ibus
-ebus
-ābus
-ōbus
